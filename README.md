# World Population Monitoring Dashboard | Power BI

Interactive Power BI dashboard analysing global population trends across 190+ countries from 1970 to 2022.

---

## Objective

To monitor and visualise global population trends across continents and countries using real-world demographic data, enabling comparison of growth rates, density patterns, and population distribution over five decades.

---

## What I Did

Collected and cleaned raw population data by handling missing values, removing duplicates, and standardising country and territory names to ensure consistency across all records. Transformed and shaped the data using Power Query, applying appropriate data types and creating calculated columns to support accurate reporting.

Built a multi-page interactive dashboard with continent and country-level slicers enabling dynamic filtering across all visuals. Created trend comparisons between 1970 and 2022 population data across top 5 countries including China, India, and the United States. Analysed population density per km² across continents, identifying Asia (1,025/km²) and Europe (663/km²) as the most densely populated regions. Visualised continental population distribution during COVID peak (2022) using pie charts, with Asia accounting for 59.48% of global population. Tracked total growth rate by continent (1970–2022), with Africa leading at 58% driven by high birth rates and improving healthcare access. Designed a sub-page with capital-level population data across 1970, 2000, 2010, and 2015 with bottom 5 country analysis.

---

## Key Findings

- Global population doubled from 4bn (1970) to 8bn (2022)
- Asia is the most populated continent, with China and India together holding 35%+ of world population
- Vatican City recorded the least populated capital in 2022
- Africa showed the highest growth rate at 58% between 1970–2022
- Monaco recorded the highest population density among all territories
- China's one-child policy visibly slowed its growth rate compared to India
